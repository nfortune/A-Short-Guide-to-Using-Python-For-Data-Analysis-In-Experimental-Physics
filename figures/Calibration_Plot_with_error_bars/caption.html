Photodiode voltage as a function of relative polarizer angle for light at 650 nm passing through two polarizers. \(V_{pd}\) corresponds to the voltage measured across a resistor placed in series with a photodiode and is linearly proportional to light intensity under these conditions. A change in polarization angle of the light (due to the Faraday effect, for example) can be detected as a change in photodiode voltage but the size of the change in voltage for a given change in polarization depends on the relative orientation of the two polarizers.  The greatest sensitivity of the photodiode to changes in polarization angle occurs when the relative angle between the two polarizers is 45 degrees.
