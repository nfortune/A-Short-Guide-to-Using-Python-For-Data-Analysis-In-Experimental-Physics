Overall, the model appears to describe the data well To answer this, we will evaluate the fit residuals and chi-square test goodness of fit. See section \ref{304456}.
Note that this was a "weighted" fit, in that the stated uncertainty of each data point was taken into account during the fit. Practically speaking, this means the curve-fitting routine tries harder to match the model to the data at points with a smaller uncertainty. This is an extra step you may not have taken before but it is essential to evaluating the 'goodness of fit' of this 'best fit' to the data, as discussed further below.
Note: there are additional features of the curve_fit function not demonstrated here, including the ability to set lower and upper bounds on each of the adjustable parameters. For example, including bounds = ([0, -np.pi,  -1.0], [np.inf, +np.pi, 1.0])
 would require \(0\ \le I_0\) , \(-\pi\le\theta_0\ \le\pi\) in rad, and \(-1\ \le\text{offset}\ \le+1\).  See the scipy.optimize.curve_fit documentation for details. 

goodness of fit

We now have a best fit of the model to the data. How good a fit is it?
Two quick tests of the goodness of fit of the model to the data — how well does the model describe the data, given a particular estimate of the uncertainty in each data point? — are calculations of the residuals and the reduced chi-square value.  See \cite{thomas2010} and \cite{herman2011}.
