We see that the curve fitting routine was able to converge, Note that this was a "weighted" fit, in that the stated uncertainty of each data point was taken into account during the fit. Practically speaking, this means the curve-fitting routine tries harder to match the model to the data at points with a smaller uncertainty. 
Note: there are additional features of the curve_fit function not demonstrated here, including the ability to set lower and upper bounds on each of the adjustable parameters. For example, including bounds = ([0, -np.pi,  -1.0], [np.inf, +np.pi, 1.0])
 would require \(0\ \le I_0\) , \(-\pi\le\theta_0\ \le\pi\) in rad, and \(-1\ \le\text{offset}\ \le+1\).  See the scipy.optimize.curve_fit documentation for details. 

goodness of fit

Two quick tests of the goodness of fit of the model to the data — how well does the model describe the data, given a particular estimate of the uncertainty in each data point? — are calculations of the residuals and the reduced chi-square value.
