Our conclusion?
Residuals: In this case, the error bars correspond to one standard deviation in the average value of 5 measurements, as obtained by a student. If the error is random, the points should be evenly and randomly distributed, with 2/3 of the points within 1 standard deviation of zero and 95% within 2 standard deviations. Overall, the residuals appear consistent with the assumption of random variation in the data.
Chi-square: According to the chi-square analysis, however, either the fit is too good or the uncertainty estimate is, on average, too large  to be true! We interpret that to mean here not that the model doesn't describe the data but that instead, the uncertainty in the data is less than reported (on average). We will revisit this later when we consider propagation of uncertainty in section \ref{393002}.

When using Python in place of a calculator, we have the ability to directly include information about dimensions, units and uncertainties in the calculations. When used, these abilities  offer the advantage of allowing us to check algebraic calculations, automatically propagate uncertainties in calculated quantities, and avoid unit conversion errors, but the necessary Python modules are not included in the standard Anaconda Navigator Python installation and must be added by hand (unless already installed as part of a specially configured webserver).
