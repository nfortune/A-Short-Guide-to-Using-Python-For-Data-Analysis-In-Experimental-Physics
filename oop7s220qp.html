Our conclusion?  The deviation of the fit from the data around 150 degrees suggests some systematic error (or fluctuation in what we were assuming was a constant background) but overall seems plausible. According to the chi-square analysis, however, the fit is too good to be true! We interpret that to mean here not that the model doesn't describe the data but that instead, we have overestimated the uncertainty in the data for most data points. We might, for example, have expected the uncertainties for most points to be similar, but that isn't what we determined experimentally.
