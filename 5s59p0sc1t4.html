An example of data smoothing using the Savitzky-Golay low pass filter is shown in Fig \ref{987576}. Significantly Increasing the window width increases the degree of smoothing, causing small features in the original 'clean signal' to disappear; significantly decreasing the window width decreasing the degree of smoothing, causing artificial features (arising from the noise) to emerge. Since for real experimental data the original 'clean signal' is unknown, the degree of smoothing is a matter of judgement! A comparithe values used for the smoothing parameters when discerning the resolution of your experiment!
